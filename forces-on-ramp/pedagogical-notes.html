📐 Forces on a Ramp

Pedagogical Notes for McKenzie Sorce's Hot Wheels F=ma Lesson

🚀 Launch Applet

🎯 Where This Came From

McKenzie's original lesson: Students used PhET simulation to explore forces on inclined planes.

Problems identified:

  • PhET sim too long — students didn't finish
  • Students procrastinated
  • Even strong students gave one-word answers
  • Weak connection to F=ma

Design goal: More focused, more visual, directly connected to physics concepts.

🛠️ Design Decisions

  • PhET-style visuals: Familiar interface reduces cognitive load
  • Draggable crate + Release: Students predict before testing (inquiry loop)
  • Dynamic vector scaling: Forces auto-scale to fit canvas at any mass/angle
  • Friction feedback message: "Too strong!" turns confusion into teaching moment
  • Energy bar (PE→KE): Real-time visualization of energy conservation
  • Side panel controls: Keeps all tools visible while manipulating

⚠️ Known Limitations

  • Angle label unclear: Degree marking doesn't clearly show what's being measured
  • No question scaffolding: Students don't know what to look for (consider sentence starters)
  • Static friction oversimplified: Real μs > μk
  • Energy bar confusion: With friction on, total energy appears to "disappear" (heat not shown)
  • No data export: Can't capture screenshots or values for analysis
  • FBD scales independently: Vectors don't match main simulation scale
