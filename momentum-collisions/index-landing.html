🎱 Momentum & Collisions Applet

A Case Study in Iterative Design

Great design isn't a flash of inspiration—it's iteration.

This applet went through three major iterations, each revealing something essential about how students actually learn physics.

Follow the journey from "physics simulation" → "feature-rich tool" → "actually usable learning experience."

🎓 The Design Journey

Each iteration taught us something we couldn't have known without building it, testing it, and watching students use it. Good educational software emerges from observation, not assumption.

This is what happens when you actually test your designs with real learners.

v3

Iteration 3: Reality Check

"Wait, students can't actually USE this..."

Current Version

What We Fixed:

  • Freeze-frame snapshots with camera shutter sound—students now know exactly when data is captured
  • 3×4 challenge grid—scannable at a glance, not an overwhelming vertical list
  • Smart color coding—subtle 18% red/15% gold backgrounds guide the eye without distraction
  • Momentum in control boxes—see each ball's momentum right where you set the speed
  • Dynamic sliders—adjust ball speed WHILE it's moving, see physics in real-time
  • Delayed auto-start—600ms grace period lets you set both balls before simulation starts
  • Discovery-based learning—momentum values hidden until you click Start, students discover conservation themselves

What We Learned:

"Direct manipulation beats buttons. Dragging a slider and seeing the velocity arrow change = concrete understanding. Let students discover patterns rather than explaining them. Test early, test often."

💡 Design Lessons for Students

This iterative journey reveals key principles of user-centered design:

  • ✦ Specs don't reveal UX problems: The "collect sample" button worked perfectly—but students didn't know WHEN to press it
  • ✦ Features can obscure purpose: 12 challenges in a vertical list felt overwhelming; a 3×4 grid is scannable
  • ✦ Direct manipulation > buttons: Dragging a slider and watching the ball move teaches more than clicking "Set Velocity"
  • ✦ Let patterns emerge: Don't TELL students "momentum is conserved"—hide the values, let them discover it
  • ✦ Test early, test often: Iteration 2 looked "complete" on paper, but 10 minutes with students revealed 7 critical UX flaws
  • ✦ Good design compounds: Each small fix (freeze-frame, color coding, dynamic sliders) reinforced the others
